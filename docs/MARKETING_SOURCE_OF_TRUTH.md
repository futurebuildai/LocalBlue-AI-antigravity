## 2. Product Overview

### The One-Liner

LocalBlue builds professional, lead-generating websites for local contractors using AI — in about 5 minutes, from a simple chat conversation.

### The Elevator Pitch (30 seconds)

"Most contractors either don't have a website or have one that's outdated and doesn't bring in leads. LocalBlue fixes that. A contractor chats with our AI for about 5 minutes — tells it about their business, their services, their service area — and the AI builds them a complete professional website. It comes with a built-in AI chatbot that captures leads 24/7, a quote calculator, and appointment booking. And the best part? They manage everything from their own domain, not ours. Their customers never see LocalBlue. It looks like a professionally designed website — because it is."

### How It Works (4-Step Story)

**Step 1: Chat with AI (5 minutes)**
The contractor has a conversation with our AI assistant. No forms to fill out, no templates to browse. They just talk about their business — what services they offer, where they work, what makes them different. The AI asks smart follow-up questions and gathers everything it needs.

**Step 2: AI Builds the Website**
In seconds, the AI generates a complete professional website with:
- Custom content written specifically for their business
- Design matched to their trade and style preference
- All their services, service areas, and business details populated
- SEO optimized for their local area

**Step 3: Manage from Their Own Domain**
The contractor gets their own admin panel at `admin.theirbusiness.com`. No need to log into LocalBlue. No visible connection to our platform. To their customers, it looks like a custom-built website.

**Step 4: Leads Flow In**
The website starts working immediately:
- An AI chatbot answers visitor questions and captures contact info 24/7
- A quote calculator lets homeowners estimate project costs
- An appointment scheduler lets them book consultations
- Contact forms send instant email alerts to the contractor

## 3. Target Audience & Personas

### Primary Persona: "Mike the Master Plumber"

Use "Mike" as shorthand in all internal discussions. He represents our core customer.

**Who Mike Is:**
- Age: 35-55
- Runs a small contracting business (1-10 employees)
- Annual revenue: $150K - $2M
- Located in suburban or urban areas
- Works 50-70 hours a week on job sites
- Technically basic — comfortable with email and smartphones, but not much beyond that
- Values his craftsmanship and reputation above everything else
- Gets most of his work through word-of-mouth and repeat customers

**What Mike Wants:**
- More quality leads (not junk leads from Thumbtack)
- To look professional when potential customers Google him
- To spend as little time as possible on marketing and admin work
- To compete with larger companies that have polished websites
- To stop worrying about his online presence

**What Frustrates Mike:**
- "I'm a plumber, not a web designer."
- "My last website cost $5,000 and still says Lorem Ipsum on one page."
- "I don't have time to figure out WordPress."
- "Every lead service I've tried sends me garbage leads and charges per lead."
- "Every website guy talks in jargon I don't understand."

**How Mike Makes Decisions:**
- Skeptical of marketing "gurus" and big promises
- Responds to proof: "Show me it works"
- Prefers straightforward, no-BS communication
- Influenced by other contractors who've had success
- Will pay for value if he can see the ROI clearly
- Prefers monthly payments (cash flow varies seasonally)

**Where to Reach Mike:**
- Facebook (personal and contractor groups)
- YouTube (watches trade tutorials)
- Trade association events and local meetups
- Supply house and distributor partnerships
- Google search ("contractor website builder", "plumber website")
- Referrals from other contractors

### Secondary Personas

**"Sarah the Solo Electrician"**
- One-person operation, does everything herself
- Even less time than Mike — needs the absolute fastest path
- Highly skeptical because she's been burned before by overpriced services
- Price sensitive but willing to pay if the value is clear

**"Carlos the Growing GC"**
- General contractor scaling from 5 to 15 employees
- Needs to look established and professional to win bigger jobs
- Interested in quote calculator and multiple service pages
- Wants team members to access the admin panel
- More receptive to the Scale plan

**"The Spouse / Office Manager"**
- Often the person who actually sets up and manages the website
- More comfortable with technology than the contractor
- Cares about ease of use and customer support
- Makes or influences the buying decision

## 9. Trade-Specific Messaging

Tailor messaging to each trade vertical. Use trade-specific language, pain points, and scenarios.

### Plumbing

**Key pain points:** Emergency calls but no online presence, losing jobs to plumbers with nice websites, customers can't find them at 2 AM
**Messaging angle:** "When a pipe bursts at midnight, homeowners Google 'emergency plumber near me.' Will they find you — or your competitor?"
**Service examples:** Drain cleaning, water heater installation, pipe repair, bathroom remodeling, sewer line work
**Image/visual themes:** Clean, professional, blue tones, water imagery

### Electrical

**Key pain points:** Safety-critical work needs trust signals, competing for panel upgrades and EV charger installations
**Messaging angle:** "Homeowners want to trust the person working on their electrical system. A professional website is the first trust signal."
**Service examples:** Panel upgrades, EV charger installation, lighting, rewiring, electrical inspections
**Image/visual themes:** Modern, technical, safety-focused

### HVAC

**Key pain points:** Seasonal demand swings, need to book in advance for busy seasons, high-ticket installations
**Messaging angle:** "When it's 95 degrees and the AC goes out, homeowners don't flip through the Yellow Pages. They search online. Be the first result."
**Service examples:** AC installation, furnace repair, duct cleaning, heat pump installation, maintenance plans
**Image/visual themes:** Comfort, temperature, family at home

### Roofing

**Key pain points:** High average ticket ($8,500+), trust is critical for big purchases, storm damage creates urgency
**Messaging angle:** "A $10,000 roofing job starts with a website visit. Your website needs to close as well as you do."
**Service examples:** Roof replacement, storm damage repair, gutters, inspections, commercial roofing
**Image/visual themes:** Before/after transformations, dramatic reveals, curb appeal

### General Contractor

**Key pain points:** Need to showcase range of services, project galleries critical, competing for remodeling jobs
**Messaging angle:** "From kitchens to additions, your portfolio should be as impressive online as it is in person."
**Service examples:** Kitchen remodels, bathroom renovations, home additions, decks, basements
**Image/visual themes:** Transformation photos, craftsmanship details, beautiful finished spaces

### Painting

**Key pain points:** Very low digital maturity, highly visual trade, color/finish expertise to showcase
**Messaging angle:** "Your work speaks for itself — but only if people can see it. A project gallery on your website does the selling for you."
**Service examples:** Interior painting, exterior painting, cabinet refinishing, deck staining, commercial painting
**Image/visual themes:** Bold color transformations, clean lines, before/after dramatic reveals

## 11. Do's and Don'ts

### Do

- Lead with benefits, not features
- Use contractor-friendly language (leads, customers, jobs, estimates)
- Show real examples of generated websites
- Emphasize the 5-minute setup and AI simplicity
- Highlight "unlimited leads" as a major differentiator
- Use before/after narratives (bad website vs. LocalBlue website)
- Acknowledge that contractors are busy and skeptical
- Include trade-specific imagery and language when targeting a vertical
- Always include a clear, single CTA
- Test messaging with actual contractors when possible

### Don't

- Use technical jargon (API, SaaS, SEO optimization, CMS, multi-tenant)
- Make specific lead count or revenue promises
- Talk down to contractors or imply they're unsophisticated
- Use the word "easy" without context (say "5 minutes" instead — it's more specific and believable)
- Show LocalBlue branding in screenshots meant to represent the contractor's experience
- Compare directly to specific competitor prices without verification
- Use stock photos of contractors when real photos are available
- Overpromise on SEO results or Google rankings
- Reference internal features, code names, or platform architecture
- Use "we" when the message should be about "you" (the contractor)
